Mississippi Chicken Meatballs

Home

Description

Capture the beloved tastes of Mississippi chicken—tangy pepperoncini, ranch, and melted Monterey Jack—in these easy-to-make meatballs, complete with a speedy sauce. For the ultimate comfort food experience, serve them over a generous bed of creamy mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground chicken
  • 1 (1 ounce) packet ranch seasoning, divided
  • 1 (1 ounce) packet au jus mix, divided
  • 1 large egg, beaten, at room temperature
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 4 pepperoncini peppers, or more to taste, diced
  • cooking spray
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons pepperoncini brine
  • 1 cup unsalted chicken broth

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Combine ground chicken, 1 1/2 tablespoons ranch seasoning, 1 1/2 tablespoon au jus mix, egg, breadcrumbs, cheese, and pepperoncini peppers in a bowl until just combined.
  3. Form 1 1/2-inch balls (about 1 tablespoon each); place on a baking sheet. Spray meatballs with cooking spray.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes; rotate meatballs halfway through baking time to brown evenly.
  5. Meanwhile, combine butter, 1/2 tablespoon ranch seasoning, 1/2 tablespoon au jus mix, pepperoncini brine, and chicken broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer, whisking constantly until reduced slightly, about 3 minutes.
  6. Remove from the oven; allow meatballs to cool for 5 to 10 minutes, Serve over mashed potatoes.
